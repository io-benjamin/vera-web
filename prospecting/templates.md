# Outreach Templates

## Email 1: Initial Contact

**Subject lines (test these):**
- Quick question about {Business Name}
- {Business Name} + online presence
- Noticed something about {Business Name}

---

**Body:**

Hi {First Name / there},

I was looking for {category} in {location} and came across {Business Name} on Google. Great reviews!

I noticed you don't have a website yet. Right now, when someone searches for you, they see your Google listing — but there's nowhere to send them to learn more, see your {menu/services/portfolio}, or contact you directly.

I build simple, professional websites for small businesses like yours. Fast turnaround (usually under a week), fair pricing, and you don't pay until you love it.

Would you be open to a quick chat about what a site could do for {Business Name}?

Either way, keep up the great work — your customers clearly love you.

Best,
{Your Name}
Vera | tryvera.dev

## Phone Script (warm call)

"Hi, is this {First Name}? 

Great — my name is {Your Name} and I actually found {Business Name} on Google the other day. You've got some really solid reviews!

I noticed you don't have a website set up yet, and I help local businesses like yours get online. Nothing crazy — just a clean, professional site so people can find you easier.

Would you have 5 minutes sometime this week to chat about it? I can show you what I've done for other businesses in the area."

---

## Key Points to Hit

1. **You found them** — feels personal, not mass email
2. **Compliment first** — reviews, reputation, something real
3. **The problem** — no website means missed opportunities
4. **The solution** — you make it easy
5. **Low risk** — don't pay until happy, quick turnaround
6. **Soft CTA** — "open to a chat" not "buy now"

## What NOT to say

- ❌ "Your competitors have websites" (threatening)
- ❌ "You're losing money" (presumptuous)
- ❌ "I can help you rank #1" (sounds like spam)
- ❌ Long paragraphs (they won't read)
- ❌ Multiple CTAs (confusing)

## Personalization Tips

- Mention something specific (a menu item, service, recent review)
- Reference the neighborhood/area
- Note how long they've been in business if visible
- If they have Instagram, mention you saw it
